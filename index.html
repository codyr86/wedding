Our Story

He was a keyboard clacking computer whiz with an insatiable passion for digital gadgets and crunchy snacks, catchy pop music, and dogs.

She was a bubbly extrovert with a new hobby each week and an uncanny ability to start and finish intricate craft projects that put Pinterest to shame.

They attended Grand Valley State University in tiny little Allendale and lived in Grand Rapids.

In those days…

Cody could be found in the geography department computer lab helping students with their map making skills and always crashing computer programs.

Claire could be found tucked away in a conference room discussing literature and leading a book club she started.

As it goes…

Both were occasionally in the same places at the same time through mutual friends.

In 2012, both freshly single and freshly happy, Cody met Claire again at a summer yard party. They sat in Adirondack chairs, legs gloriously spread to the sun, with cold beers in their hands. They will argue and say this never happened, but I remember it and I remember thinking that they looked good like that.

From them on…

His name would come up in conversation. Hers would too.

They shared one friend in particular (hey there!) who thought this would be the perfect time to meddle up some love between two great people.

So on January 17th 2013...

Cody, Claire, and some of their friends forged through the winter vortex and settled into the cozy Monarch Bar on Grand Rapids' West Side for some cheap beer, fooseball, and truth-or-dare-Jenga.

Cody sat next to Claire, which happened to be a very good thing because late into the night he pulled out the Jenga piece that said "Kiss the person to your left".

Without hesitation, and without crashing the Jenga tower, Cody kissed Claire.

From that day on...

It was Claire matching her gourmet culinary skills with Cody's bottomless pit of a stomach. It was Breaking Bad marathons. It was Cody introducing Claire to the joys of four season backpacking.

It was endless summer bike rides. It was saying I love you. It was sharing holidays and family meals and trips with friends. It was talking about the M word. It was moving away from their roommates and moving in with each other. It was the best of times and the worst of times and now...

It is wedding time!
